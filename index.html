Culture in Tanzania

Definition · Aspects & Elements · Diversity · Positive & Negative Practices · Promotion · Preventive Care

National Exam Style Questions Included (CSEE)
1. Definition of Culture

Culture refers to the total way of life of a particular group of people. It includes their beliefs, customs, values, language, arts, laws, knowledge, and habits that are learned and shared from one generation to another.

“Culture is the collective programming of the mind that distinguishes members of one group from another.”

Key characteristics: Culture is learned, shared, symbolic, dynamic, and integrated. It shapes identity and social behaviour.

2. Aspects & Elements of Culture

Aspects of culture refer to broad categories or dimensions:

  • Material culture: physical objects – tools, clothing, houses, technology.
  • Non-material culture: beliefs, values, norms, language, rituals.
📌 Elements of culture (core components):
  • Language: Kiswahili as national language, tribal languages (e.g., Sukuma, Chaga, Maasai).
  • Religion & Beliefs: Christianity, Islam, African traditional beliefs.
  • Cuisine: Ugali, nyama choma, pilau, ndizi, local vegetables.
  • Arts, Music & Dance: Ngoma dances, Tinga Tinga paintings, Bongo Flava.
  • Customs & Norms: Greetings (Shikamoo), respect for elders, communal work (Ujamaa spirit).
  • Dress: Kitenge, Khanga, Kanzu, traditional Maasai shuka.
3. Cultural Diversity in Tanzania

Tanzania has over 120 ethnic groups, each with unique traditions, languages, and customs. Major groups include: Sukuma, Nyamwezi, Chaga, Haya, Maasai, Zaramo, and many more.

Examples of diversity:
- Maasai: Pastoralism, unique dress, jumping dance (adumu).
- Chaga: Banana farming, kihamba system, traditional ngoma.
- Zanzibar: Swahili architecture, taarab music, Islamic influences.
- Sukuma: Largest ethnic group, famous for bugobobobo dance.

Importance of diversity: National unity (Umoja), tourism attraction, cultural exchange, and enrichment of national identity. The government promotes unity through “Unity in Diversity” (Umoja katika Utofauti).

4. Positive & Negative Cultural Practices

Positive Practices

  • Respect for elders (Shikamoo)
  • Communal work (Ujamaa, Nyakitontono)
  • Hospitality and generosity
  • Strong family ties & lineage
  • Preserving traditional medicine & knowledge
  • Rites of passage (positive guidance)

Negative Practices

  • Female Genital Mutilation (FGM)
  • Child marriage
  • Witchcraft accusations
  • Gender discrimination in inheritance
  • Albino killings (myth-based)
  • Excessive bride price (dowry burden)
Civic responsibility: Tanzanian laws (Sexual Offences Special Provisions Act, Anti-FGM laws) prohibit harmful practices. Citizens must report and eradicate negative traditions.
5. Promotion of National Culture

The Tanzanian government promotes national culture through:

  • National festivals: Nane Nane (farmers' day), Saba Saba, Independence Day celebrations showcasing cultural dances.
  • Cultural institutions: National Arts Council (BASATA), National Museum, Bagamoyo College of Arts.
  • Education curriculum: Teaching Kiswahili and cultural values in schools.
  • Media & Arts: Promoting local music (Bongo Flava, traditional ngoma) through radio and TV.
  • Cultural tourism: Maasai bomas, Zanzibar Stone Town, tribal festivals.
Student role: Participate in cultural clubs, wear kitenge with pride, learn traditional dances, respect national symbols and heritage.
6. Preventive Care & Maintenance of Property

Culture of preventive care means having a mindset and practice of regularly maintaining personal and public property to avoid damage, prolong lifespan, and save resources.

📌 Examples of preventive care culture:
  • Cleaning classrooms, hospitals, and markets (public hygiene).
  • Repairing leaking taps to save water.
  • Regular servicing of school desks, computers, and tools.
  • Planting trees and protecting environment.
  • Proper use of roads, traffic lights, and public latrines.

Importance: Reduces government expenditure, ensures sustainability, builds civic responsibility, and promotes national development. “Kila Mtanzania aweze kulinda na kutunza mali ya umma.”

National Exam Style Questions
CSEE Civics | Click to reveal answers
📝 1. Define culture and explain any four elements of culture. (5 marks)
Answer: Culture is the total way of life of a people including beliefs, customs, arts, etc.
Elements: Language (Kiswahili), Religion (Christianity/Islam), Cuisine (ugali), Arts (ngoma music), Customs (respect for elders), Dress (kitenge) — any 4 explained.
📝 2. Show how cultural diversity in Tanzania promotes national unity. (4 marks)
Answer: Tanzania has over 120 ethnic groups living peacefully. Diversity promotes national unity through: intermarriages, shared language (Kiswahili), national festivals, respect for all tribes, and the policy of "Unity in Diversity".
📝 3. Identify three negative cultural practices in Tanzania and suggest solutions. (6 marks)
Answer: Negative practices: FGM, child marriage, witchcraft accusations. Solutions: enforce anti-FGM laws, educate communities on dangers, provide alternative rites of passage, empower girls through education.
📝 4. Explain the importance of promoting national culture in Tanzania. (5 marks)
Answer: Promotes national identity, attracts tourism, preserves heritage, strengthens patriotism, reduces cultural erosion from globalization, and instills moral values in youth.
📝 5. What is "culture of preventive care"? Give three examples. (4 marks)
Answer: Preventive care culture is the habit of maintaining property regularly to avoid damage. Examples: repairing school desks, cleaning public toilets, servicing water pumps, planting trees, conserving electricity.
📝 6. Distinguish between material and non-material culture. (3 marks)
Answer: Material culture includes physical objects (houses, tools, clothes). Non-material culture includes beliefs, values, norms, language, and traditions. Both form the complete cultural system.
📝 7. Discuss four positive cultural practices in Tanzania that should be preserved. (8 marks)
Answer: 1) Respect for elders (Shikamoo) — strengthens social harmony. 2) Communal work (Ujamaa) — boosts development. 3) Traditional dances — preserve heritage. 4) Hospitality — attracts tourists. Any other relevant.
📝 8. Why is it important for Tanzanian youth to maintain a culture of preventive care? (4 marks)
Answer: It reduces waste of public funds, develops sense of responsibility, prolongs lifespan of infrastructure, and builds a self-reliant nation as per Arusha Declaration principles.
📝 9. How does globalization threaten Tanzanian culture? (Necta style) (5 marks)
Answer: Globalization leads to cultural erosion through foreign media (Western values), loss of language, change in dressing (westernization), decline of traditional music, and weakening of local customs. Youth adopt foreign lifestyles.
📝 10. Mention three government strategies to promote national culture. (3 marks)
Answer: 1) Establishing cultural centers (Bagamoyo). 2) Supporting BASATA to regulate arts. 3) Including cultural lessons in school curriculum. 4) Celebrating Nane Nane and cultural festivals.
