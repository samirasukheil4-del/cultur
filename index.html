📖 Definition & Major Aspects of Globalization

Globalization is the process of increasing integration and interdependence among countries through cross-border flows of goods, services, capital, technology, information, and people. It links local events to global forces.

Economic: Global trade, FDI, MNCs, supply chains.

Social/Cultural: Spread of ideas, music, values, digital communities.

Political: International bodies (UN, WTO, AU), global treaties.

Technological: Internet, mobile phones, global knowledge sharing.

Environmental: Cross-border climate challenges and cooperation.

Tanzania in focus: Globalization connects Tanzanian farmers to world markets (coffee, cashew nuts, gold) and brings foreign investments like digital infrastructure.

⚙️ Driving Forces of Globalization

  • Technological revolution: Internet, mobile phones (M-Pesa in Tanzania), low-cost transport.
  • Trade liberalization: WTO agreements, EAC integration, reduced tariffs.
  • Global financial systems: Capital mobility, foreign direct investment.
  • Transportation efficiency: Container shipping linking Dar es Salaam port.
  • Multinational corporations (MNCs): Coca-Cola, Unilever, etc.
  • Global media & digital connectivity: Social media, global news.

📉 Structural Adjustment Programs (SAPs) – Definition, Objectives, Criticism & Achievements

📌 Definition: SAPs are economic policies imposed by IMF and World Bank on developing countries (including Tanzania in 1980s–90s) as loan conditions: privatization, deregulation, trade liberalization, reduced government spending.
🎯 Objectives: Reduce deficits & inflation; export-led growth; privatize state firms; attract FDI.
⚠️ Criticisms: Deepened poverty; cuts in health/education; loss of sovereignty; deindustrialization.
🏆 Achievements: Stabilized hyperinflation; fiscal discipline; boosted exports in some nations.
SAPs in Tanzania: Under President Mwinyi, reforms included devaluation of shilling, removing price controls, privatization. While inflation reduced, social hardships emerged (retrenchments, reduced health budgets). Later, Tanzania adopted poverty reduction strategies (MKUKUTA).

🌍 Importance & Effects of Globalization on Tanzania

✅ Positive Effects

  • Access to international markets for cash crops (coffee, cashew nuts).
  • Foreign investment in mining, tourism, telecom (Vodacom, Airtel).
  • Mobile money revolution (M-Pesa, Tigo Pesa).
  • Infrastructure projects financed by global partners (SGR railway).
  • Technology transfer & improved health partnerships.

⚠️ Negative Effects (incl. environment)

  • Deindustrialization: local textile industries collapsed due to cheap imports.
  • Income inequality & urban-rural gap.
  • Carbon emissions from global transport, deforestation for exports.
  • Overfishing by foreign vessels & plastic pollution.
  • Cultural erosion: Western media influence.
Environmental focus in Tanzania: Climate change affects agriculture (unpredictable rains in Arusha, Kilimanjaro); rising sea levels threaten Dar es Salaam. Global cooperation also brings renewable energy investments.
